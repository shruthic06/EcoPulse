💡 What does this mean?

Polyester has lower water usage but higher carbon emissions and health risks compared to natural fibers.

🌍

⚠️ Health Risks

🔴
Microplastic shedding — polyester releases thousands of plastic microfibers per wash cycle that enter waterways and the food chain.
🟠
Skin irritation — synthetic fibers trap heat and moisture against skin, increasing risk of dermatitis and allergic reactions.
🟠
Chemical residues — polyester production involves antimony trioxide, a potential carcinogen, which can remain in finished fabric.
🔴
Poor breathability — 100% synthetic blends reduce airflow, creating conditions for bacterial and fungal growth with extended wear.
